# Special Relativity in Financial Modeling

## Introduction
This project introduces **a third-dimensional approach to financial modeling** by incorporating **principles of special relativity** to account for dynamic changes in financial markets. Traditional models analyze price movements and volatility over time, but they often fail to account for **velocity and acceleration of market changes**â€”key factors in high-frequency trading and macroeconomic shifts.

## Problem Statement
Conventional financial models operate within a **static, linear time framework**, treating price movements as independent fluctuations rather than interdependent changes influenced by the speed and acceleration of market forces. This limits their ability to predict **rapid shifts and compounding effects** over time.

By integrating **special relativity**, we introduce a **spatial-temporal framework** where asset prices, market momentum, and economic signals are modeled in a non-linear system that better represents real-world financial behavior.

## Methodology
This project applies:
- **Lorentz Transformations** to adjust for the relative speed of financial movements.
- **Time Dilation Concepts** to weight financial indicators dynamically based on the velocity of change.
- **Four-Dimensional Market Representation** where assets exist in a space-time continuum rather than just a two-dimensional price/time chart.
- **Tensor Calculus & Differential Equations** to map multi-variable dependencies in market conditions.

This model enables **real-time adaptation** to financial fluctuations, prioritizing rapid acceleration in price movements over static price changes.
